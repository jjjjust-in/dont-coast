5,500 miles. 20 days each way. One continuous effort.

Departing May 23, 2026

Don't Coast is a personal project built around forward motion — as much a creative pursuit as it is a physical one. It started on the bike as a reminder to keep pedaling and stay engaged, and has since become a way of approaching work, process, and long-term ideas.

Cycling has been a constant in my life and a primary source of inspiration. The repetition, the terrain, and the time spent moving through landscapes all shape how I think and create. That connection carries directly into work across writing, photography, video, and design.

Much of this is currently centered around the Tour Divide, where I document the miles, the gear, and the people along the way.

Pay The Other Way

The Tour Divide is often called the most expensive "free" bike race in the world. Beyond the bike and gear, the real cost shows up day by day, around $75 to keep moving. Gas station food, the occasional hot meal, a motel bed every few days, and whatever it takes to keep the bike rolling across 2,700 miles of remote terrain. It adds up quickly.

I'm launching this site the day I roll out of Antelope Wells. From there, it's roughly 20 days north to Banff for the Grand Depart, then straight back south to where it all started. 40 days total.

There are better places to put your money, no question. But if following along brings you something, and you feel like sending a little fuel my way, it goes a long way out here. A coffee, a Red Bull, a pack of peanut butter cups. It all counts. If the ride resonates, you can support it below.
